## 1. Introduction

Rapid industrialization and global economic development of modern 
society have caused enormous pressure on ecological environment and 
water resource [1,2].Massive quantities of wastewater discharged from 
both daily life and industries every day have become the main reason of 
the water pollution and the deterioration of ecological environment 
[3,4], also putting the public health at risk [5,6]. It is reported that by 
the year of 2020, the daily treatment capacity of wastewater treatment 
facilities has been elevated to 50 million m3 [7]. There is a formidable 
challenge to the economic development since it is estimated that the 
entire human race will face a 40 % water shortage by the year of 2030 
[8]. Worse still, modern industries and human activities have been 
accelerating the global warming effect due to the immoderate con­
sumption of fossil fuel and uncontrolled emission of greenhouse gases.

carbon and greenhouse gases. In other word, more energy consumed

## 8. Conclusions

This study summarized the potential of bioenergy and bioresource 
recovery from the carbon-neutral treatment of wastewater with the 
following key findings: 
1) Anaerobic biotechnologies are conformed with the principle of car­
bon neutrality due to their availability in energy conservation and 
carbon reduction during the wastewater treatment process. State-of- 
the-art anaerobic bioprocesses and enhanced strategies are recog­
nized as the suitable solutions to the carbon-neutral treatment of 
both high-strength and low-strength wastewater. 
2) Enhancement strategies like co-digestion, co-culture, biochar addi­
tion and hydrogen fermentation are also recommended to improve 
and optimize anaerobic biotechnologies from the aspects of 
enhancing the performance of energy production and carbon 
reduction.  
3) Sustainable and low-carbon biological nitrogen removal processes 
like SAD and anammox are remarkable biotechnologies which also 
accord with the carbon neutrality. Anammox-HAP process is also an 
effective way to deal with both nitrogen removal and phosphorus 
recovery. 
4) Future concern should be focused on the development of the inte­
gration of AnMBR and anammox process for both treatment of 
organic and inorganic pollutants.
