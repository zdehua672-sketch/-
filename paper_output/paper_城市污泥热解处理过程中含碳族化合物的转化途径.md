## 1. Introduction

With urbanization progressing, the rapid increase in municipal 
sewage discharge has led to a corresponding growth in the volume of 
municipal sludge (MS) generated from wastewater treatment (Marouˇsek 
et al., 2022). The annual global production of MS is on average 45 
million dry tons (Andr´e et al., 2023). There has been an increase in the 
production of MS in recent years. In China, the annual municipal sludge 
production exceeded 60 million tons (calculated at 80 % moisture 
content) in 2020 (Zhang et al., 2023). It is expected to exceed 103 
million tons (at 80 % moisture content) by 2025 (Zan et al., 2023). 
Municipal sludge contains high levels of organic contaminants, heavy 
metals, pathogens, and other potentially harmful substances, which can 
pollute soils and groundwater and negatively impact air quality if not 
properly treated, posing a significant threat to the environment. Thus, it 
is important to develop efficient and eco-friendly sludge treatment 
technologies to safely and effectively dispose of sludge. On the other 
hand, municipal sludge also contains a large number of carbon re­
sources, with an average organic carbon content of 222.74 g/kg in China 
(Moldes et al., 2007; Zhu et al., 2017). It could yield over 13.36 million 
tons of carbon per year if it is fully recycled, making it a valuable 
resource. Therefore, it is important to focus not only on developing 
efficient sludge disposal technologies but also on utilizing the carbon 
obtained from sludge as a resource to support the national dual carbon 
policy.

resource recovery (Zhang et al., 2021). It has received extensive atten­

## 2. Materials and methods

2.1. Materials characterization 
Sludge samples were collected from a municipal wastewater treat­
ment plant in China. The municipal sludge (MS) samples were air dried 
for two days and then placed in a drying oven at 105 ℃ until they 
reached a constant weight. The dried sludge samples were ground into 
particles smaller than 0.15 mm and placed in the dryer until use. 
2.2. Pyrolysis of MS 
For the MS pyrolysis process (shown in Fig. S1), 6.00 g of MS was 
loaded into a quartz container and pyrolyzed at temperatures of 200 ℃, 
300 ℃, 400 ℃, 500 ℃, 600 ℃, and 700 ℃, with a heating rate of 10 ◦C/ 
min. During the process, argon was used as the carrier gas at a flow rate 
of 40 mL/min, and the retention time was 40 min. Pyrolysis products 
were obtained at different pyrolysis temperatures and cooled to room 
temperature under argon. The pyrolysis gas flow was recorded through a 
wet flow meter and collected in a gas collection bag, and the pyrolysis 
liquid was recovered through a condenser. After the pyrolysis furnace 
cooled, the residual solids in the tube were weighed, and the output of 
the final three-phase product was on ash-free ‘af’ basis. The pyrolysis 
products were calculated using the following equations (Li et al., 2020; 
He et al., 2018):

2.3. Residual solids analysis 
The protein content in the MS and pyrolysis products was deter­
mined using a BCA reagent kit (Solarbio, Beijing, China) (Ras et al., 
2008). The cellulose and hemicellulose contents were determined using 
a cellulose and hemicellulose reagent kit (Solarbio, Beijing, China) 
(Gupta et al., 2018; Qi et al., 2020). The procedures for determining the 
protein, cellulose, hemicellulose content and the organic content of 
municipal sludge are described in Text S1. The determination of lipids 
was carried out using a Soxhlet extractor Model B-811 produced by 
Buchi.

## 4. Conclusion

Pyrolysis technology is an effective way to reduce the mass of sludge. 
In this study, the transformation pathways of carbon-containing group 
compounds and the mechanisms behind the mass reduction in sludge by 
pyrolysis were explored. The masses of proteins, cellulose, and hemi­
cellulose decreased significantly during the process, and the pyrolysis of 
these carbon-containing group compounds was almost complete at 
temperatures of 600 ◦C (protein), 400 ◦C (cellulose), and 300 ◦C 
(hemicellulose). In the pyrolysis gas, CO2 was the most prevalent 
carbonaceous gas, accounting for over 69 % of the total gases (CO2, CO, 
CH4) produced throughout the process. The yield of pyrolysis liquid was 
low in the low-temperature stage (30–300 ◦C). However, it contained a 
certain amount of substance, such as aliphatics, that can be recycled. 
The yield of unfavorable carbon-containing substances for resource 
utilization (OOSs, ONSs) was relatively low at 200 ◦C. The yield of py­
rolysis liquid and gas increased as the temperature increased in the 
medium-temperature stage, whereas the liquid and gas are not condu­
cive to resource utilization. The yield of CO that are resource-utilizable 
gas in the pyrolysis gas rapidly increased as the temperature increased in 
the high-temperature stage (500–700 ◦C). This study confirms the 
research hypothesis and provides insights into the resourceful conver­
sion and environmentally friendly disposal of municipal sludge in in­
dustry. However, small-scale experiments and a single source of samples 
provide insufficient support for further commercial applications. Addi­
tional experiments are also needed to validate material transformation 
relationships. Therefore, conducting experiments on a larger scale and 
with a broader range of sample sources is necessary. Further in­
vestigations into intricate mechanisms, process optimization and auto­
mation of managerial decisions using computer technology, along with 
material and energy flow analyses, as well as financial analyses during 
the MS pyrolysis process, are also needed.
