## Abstract

The fact that an elevated discharge in greenhouse gas concentration results in global 
warming by human activities, defined as greenhouse effect, has been a toppest issue.The 
suite of Sewage Sludge treatment is considered as an important source of greenhouse gas 
discharge.The amount of microorganism lived in sewage-transpoted biofilms could not 
only change water quality, but also discharge a mass of greenhouse gas in urban sewage 
pipe network. However, few studies have focused on quantitative evaluation of sewage-
transported greenhouse gas in urban sewage pipe network. In this study, we monitored 
the variation of greenhouse gas concentration in urban sewage pipe network (the total 
length of 839km) in Xi’an from 2014 to 2017, and conducted a quantative evaluation of 
greenhouse gas discharge based upon carbon footprint analysis. 
The results show that average CO2 concentrations in branch pipes, main pines and 
leading pipes are 5916 mg/L, 2871 mg/L and 1830 mg/L, respectively, and average CH4 
concentrations are 2937 mg/L, 1445 mg/L and 914 mg/L, respectively. The total lengths 
in branch pipes, main pipes and leading pipes are 247521 m, 219506 m and 371873 m, 
respectively, and the diameters are 1000-2000 mm and 400- 800 mm and 250-500 mm, 
respectively, and the average flow velositiesare 0.0027 m3/s, 0.027 m3/s and 0.045 m3/s, 
respectively. Therefore,the total amount of CO2 discharge is 15.07 t/d, the total amount 
of CH4 is 7.50 t/d in urban sewage pipe network in X’an. Combined with the influence 
factors of CO2 and CH4 on the greenhouse effect(1:23), so the total CO2 discharge is 
187.59 t/d.
